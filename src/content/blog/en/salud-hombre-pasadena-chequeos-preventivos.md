Many Hispanic men in Pasadena and the Houston area only see a doctor when the pain won't let them work anymore. The problem is that conditions like high blood pressure, diabetes and prostate issues **progress silently for years** without symptoms. By the time discomfort shows up, the damage is done. The good news: a preventive checkup takes less than an hour and can catch these problems early.

## Why should men get regular checkups?

- High blood pressure and high cholesterol **don't hurt**, but they slowly damage your heart and kidneys.
- Type 2 diabetes can go undiagnosed for years; catching it early prevents serious complications.
- Prostate cancer found early is far simpler and more effective to treat.
- Low testosterone explains fatigue, low mood and poor sleep that many men just accept as normal.
- A blood test and a general exam take less than an hour, once a year.

A blood test that measures prostate-specific antigen. It's the simplest way to monitor prostate health starting at age 40–50, or earlier with a family history.

## Warning signs: don't ignore them

Come to the clinic as soon as possible if you experience:

- Chest pain or pressure, or shortness of breath with exertion
- Difficulty urinating, weak stream or blood in the urine
- Excessive thirst, constant hunger and frequent urination
- Extreme fatigue that doesn't improve with rest
- Unexplained weight loss
- Frequent headaches with high blood pressure
- Erection problems (they can be an early sign of a circulation issue)

## Practical tips to take care of your health

- Get a general checkup **once a year**, even if you feel fine.
- Know your numbers: blood pressure, glucose and cholesterol.
- Cut back on sodas and fried food; walk at least 30 minutes a day.
- Don't normalize constant fatigue, poor sleep or irritability.
- If you smoke, get help quitting: your cardiac risk drops within the first month.
